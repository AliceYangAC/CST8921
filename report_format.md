# Title

## Introduction or Purpose

- Objectives or purpose of the lab. (why you did it, hypothesis) 
- Background information
- Briefly summarize how the lab was performed, state the findings of the lab, and list the conclusions of the investigation. 

## Steps covered in the lab

Describe the steps you completed during your investigation. This is your procedure. Be
sufficiently detailed that anyone could read this section and duplicate your lab. Write it as if you were giving direction for someone else to do the lab. It may be helpful to provide a figure to diagram your lab setup.

## Results

Describe in words what the data means. Sometimes the Results section is combined with the
Discussion.

## Discussion or Analysis or Conculsion

- Present your findings from the lab.
- Evaluate the outcome objectively, taking a candid and unbiased point of view. Suppose that
the outcome is not close to what you expected. Even then, after checking your results, give
reasons why you believe that outcome is not consistent with the expected.
- State the discrepancies between the results and the (theory)
- Suggest improvements

## References

If your research was based on someone else's work or if you cited facts that require
documentation, then you should list these references.
